Kingsman : The Secret Service

By Matthew Vaughn

Chapter 1

Eggsy is introduced as quick-witted and capable. Although, he is also shown to be held back by his environment and how often he chooses to sabotage himself. It shows that he believes he's better than his situation, but also establishes his confidence and loyalty.

Chapter 2

Continuing the narative that Eggsy is lost, he actually triggers the emergency contact that his father had left for him. It feels unreal until it actually works. All of sudden it feels like there is an answer for him. Eggsy is quite suspicious of the situation, but you can also sense hope underneath it all.

Chapter 3

Galahad then steps into Eggsy's life, in which he reads Eggsy instantly. He tests his composure, intelligence, and instincts. Galahad also reveals what the "Kingsman" really are for, that they aren't just cool and fancy spies, but serious agents who constantly teeter between the line of life and death. Eggsy's pride and ego is challenged, realizing he isn't the only talented person there and that his raw skill isn't enough.

Chapter 4

After these tests, Eggsy is offered a place into the Kingsman. Although not a tradtional offer, he is thrown "straight to the wolves" and has to thrive in a new world he hasn't seen before. Navigating between a world of new rules, and hidden violence. This is where Eggsy shows his first real signs of a goal. He's still suspicious and exhibiting defense behaviors, but he is now aiming for something higher than just surviving.
